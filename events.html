Festivals We Celebrate

Gopashtami

Karthika Sukla Asthami

January

The most auspicious day for cow worship, celebrating Lord Krishna's divine connection with cows. Our sacred mothers are beautifully decorated with flowers, tilaka, and bells. Special prayers, aarti, and community feasts mark this blessed celebration.

Activities: Cow decoration, special prasadam, community prayers, cultural programs

Makar Sankranti

January 14th

Harvest festival honoring cows and bulls for their contribution to agriculture. Cows are painted with vibrant colors, adorned with flower garlands, and fed special delicacies made from jaggery, rice, and coconut. A celebration of gratitude and abundance.

Activities: Colorful cow painting, traditional sweets, kite flying, cultural dance

Holi Celebration

Festival of Colors

March

Celebrating the divine play of colors with our sacred mothers. Natural, organic colors are gently applied to cows as part of the festivities. Community gathering with traditional music, dance, and sharing of joy with both humans and cows..

Activities: Natural color celebration, traditional music, community feast, cow blessing

Akshaya Tritiya

Festival of Wealth and Prosperity

April

The golden day for new beginnings and prosperity. Cows are worshipped for bringing abundance and good fortune. Special gold-colored decorations, premium food offerings, and prayers for the well-being of all living beings.

Activities: Golden decorations, prosperity prayers, special feeding, community blessings.

Janmashtami

Krishna Janma Celebration

August

Celebrating Lord Krishna's birth with midnight prayers and cow blessings. The entire goshala is illuminated with diyas, and cows are honored as Krishna's beloved companions. Devotional songs, dance performances, and community celebrations.

Activities: Midnight aarti, devotional music, traditional drama, prasadam distribution.

Karthika Purnima

Full Moon in the Month of Kartika

November

Sacred full moon night dedicated to spiritual purification and cow blessings. Evening prayers under moonlight, oil lamp ceremonies around the goshala, and special night-time feeding for our beloved cows with their favorite treats.

Activities: Moonlight prayers, oil lamp ceremony, special night feeding, meditation.
